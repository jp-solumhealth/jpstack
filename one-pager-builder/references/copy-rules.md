# Copy Rules

These rules are non-negotiable. They are what make the brief land with a C-suite reader in 20 seconds.

## Tone

- **Peer-to-peer.** JP is a CEO writing to another CEO. Not a vendor pitching a buyer.
- **Confident and direct.** Short sentences. No hedging. No "may", "might", "could potentially".
- **Earns every line.** If a sentence is not doing work, cut it.

## Forbidden

### Em dashes
No em dashes (—). They are the biggest AI-writing tell. Use a period or a comma instead.

- Bad: "Solum runs the workflows — referrals, intake, verification."
- Good: "Solum runs the workflows. Referrals, intake, verification."

En dashes in number ranges are fine: `$15–25K`, `10–30 visits`.
Hyphens in compound words are fine: `multi-location`, `front-office`, `write-offs`.

### Buzzwords (instant C-suite disqualifiers)
- "leverage"
- "synergy"
- "unlock"
- "empower"
- "partner" / "partnership" (as a verb)
- "journey"
- "solution"
- "enable"
- "world-class"
- "next-generation"

### Vendor language
- "we help you" → just state what Solum does
- "our platform" → just "Solum"
- "we would love to" → cut entirely
- "let's explore" → cut entirely

## Required

### Lead with the stake
The hero headline must contain the network dollar figure. A cold C-suite reader gives the top third of the page a 20-second read. If the money is not visible in that window, they do not keep reading.

- Good: "$20–35M a year is sitting in [target]'s front offices."
- Bad: "Annie, Solum's AI coordinator, runs your front office across all 500 locations."

### Size of prize ordering
Order the five opportunities largest-dollar first. Do not use a narrative flow (referral → verification → auth). CEOs anchor on the first number they see.

### Peer close
Sign off with a low-friction, high-conviction CTA. Examples:
- "Happy to walk the model. 30 minutes, peer to peer."
- "If the numbers hold, the next step is yours."

Never: "Schedule a demo", "Start a pilot", "Partner with us".

## Conservative numbers

ALWAYS anchor to the prospect's estimated system revenue. Never exceed 5–10% of total revenue as combined annual upside.

If the prospect is $300M system revenue:
- Combined upside ceiling: $15–30M
- Split: ~$8–13M recaptured + ~$12–22M saved

If the prospect is $1B system revenue:
- Combined upside ceiling: $50–100M
- Same split ratio

Sanity check before sending: if any single opportunity exceeds 3% of total revenue, cut it.

## The five opportunity framing

Each card is ONE sentence of description. More than that fights the layout and breaks the "clean" read.

Pattern:
```
[Observation about their current state]. [What Solum does about it].
```

Examples:
- "Auth work eats close to 1 FTE per clinic. Solum submits, tracks, and escalates automatically."
- "15–25% of inbound referrals never land on a calendar. Solum books the eval before it ages out."

## Franchise / scale thesis

If the prospect is multi-location, franchise, or roll-up, include a thesis callout between the opportunities and the totals band. The thesis is about consistency at scale, not about Solum.

Pattern:
- Open with what breaks at scale: "The front office is where the playbook breaks."
- Name their moat: "[Target]'s moat is replicable unit economics across 500+ locations."
- Name what Solum collapses: "Solum collapses that variance. One operational layer corporate owns."
- Close with a thesis line: "What you standardize, you scale."

## Numbers you can use without citation

These are defensible PT/therapy industry benchmarks. For other verticals, confirm with the user before using.

- 15–25% of inbound referrals never get scheduled
- 2–3% of PT net revenue lost to bad verifications
- 0.5–1 FTE/clinic on prior auth work
- 20–30% of front-desk time is automatable admin
- PT episodes: 10–30 visits per plan

## Claims that need user verification

Flag these for the user to confirm before sending:
- Specific customer savings numbers ("$247K avg" — verify it is per-company, not per-location)
- Specific customer names (confirm permission to name-drop)
- EBITDA multiple claims ("nine-figure enterprise value")
- Location-count claims (franchise counts move — verify current)
