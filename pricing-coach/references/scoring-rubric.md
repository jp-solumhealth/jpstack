# Pricing Coach Scoring Rubric

## Score: 5 — Pricing Nailed It

The pricing conversation directly led to a conversion action.

**Must meet at least one:**
- Prospect agreed to start (onboarding, BAA signed, trial activated)
- Prospect explicitly said pricing is acceptable ("not a big deal", "let's do it", "sounds good")
- Free trial was offered AND accepted on the same call

**Bonus indicators:**
- "Show, Then Price" sequence was followed (demo/results before pricing)
- Prospect didn't need clarification on what they're paying for
- Implementation timeline was stated
- No setup fee friction

**Benchmark examples:**
- Ruby ABA Assessment (Feb 5): Free test + "$500 is nothing to get started"
- A Step At A Time (Feb 24): Free tier + clear upgrade path → converted in 2 days
- Cedar Grove ABA (Feb 24): Free account → immediate adoption

---

## Score: 4 — Pricing Went Well

Pricing was discussed clearly, prospect understood, and a concrete next step was set.

**Must meet all:**
- Pricing was discussed on the call (not deferred)
- Prospect understood the price without repeated clarification
- A specific next step was scheduled (not "I'll think about it")

**Typical indicators:**
- Prospect asked follow-up questions about features at that price (engagement, not confusion)
- Pricing was presented after a demo or results review
- Free trial offered on first call
- Prospect referenced the price positively in context of value

**Benchmark examples:**
- MBH Services (Feb 11): $500/mo + trial → requested trial access
- Pomm RI (Feb 17): Free tier + $500 mention → testing free tier
- Grateful Care ABA (Feb 25): Free assessment → team agreed to test

---

## Score: 3 — Pricing Was Okay, Not Great

Pricing was discussed but with some friction, confusion, or neutral outcome.

**Typical indicators:**
- Prospect needed 1-2 clarifying questions about pricing (not a deal-breaker)
- Next step is vague ("I'll review internally", "let me talk to my team")
- Pricing was discussed but not the primary focus
- No conversion action but no negative signals either
- Product naming caused minor confusion that was resolved

**Benchmark examples:**
- Horizons ABA (Feb 5): Info + pilot offer → interested but evaluating
- BH Auth FUP (Feb 6): Per-document pricing → pending stakeholder
- Hillside FUP (Feb 10): $2,000/mo → 60-day internal review

---

## Score: 2 — Pricing Created Problems

Pricing caused friction, confusion, or pushback that may harm the deal.

**Indicators (any one triggers a 2):**
- Prospect expressed confusion about pricing structure ("I don't understand the model")
- Prospect pushed back on per-unit pricing at scale
- Multiple rounds of clarification needed (3+ questions about pricing)
- Prospect explicitly said the pricing model doesn't work for them
- Product naming confusion consumed significant call time
- Minimum fee concept caused confusion
- No next step was set despite pricing discussion

**Benchmark violations typically present:**
- Per-unit pricing presented to enterprise prospect (1,000+ clients)
- 12+ line items shown without a summary tier
- "Minimum fee" framing used instead of "plan includes credits"
- Pricing shown before results/demo

**Benchmark examples:**
- Attain ABA FUP #1 (Feb 16): Per-unit at enterprise scale → overwhelmed

---

## Score: 1 — Pricing Failed or Was Missed

Pricing either created a serious problem or never happened at all.

**Indicators (any one triggers a 1):**
- Pricing was never discussed (ran out of time)
- Prospect explicitly asked for pricing and didn't get it
- Pricing was promised to be "sent later" without any discussion
- Prospect objected to pricing and no resolution was attempted
- Deal appears stalled specifically because of pricing
- Enterprise prospect has been waiting 7+ days for a pricing proposal

**Benchmark examples:**
- Storybook ABA (Feb 17): "What is the monthly price?" → "I have a hard stop"
- Attain ABA FUP #2 (Feb 24): Still no engagement letter after 2 follow-ups

## Benchmark Violation Flags

Flag these independently of score. A call can score 4 but still have a violation.

| Violation | What Happened | Correct Play |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **No Free Offer** | First call with prospect, no free trial/test offered | Always offer free test on first call (86% win rate) |
| **Price Before Value** | Pricing discussed before showing results on prospect's data | Show their data processed first, then price |
| **Line Item Overload** | 5+ individual price points presented | Lead with plan tier ($500 or $2,500), overage table only if asked |
| **Name Confusion** | Prospect confused by self-check/initial check/eligibility check | Use: Quick Check, Auto VOB, Deep VOB |
| **Minimum Fee Framing** | Said "minimum fee" instead of "plan includes credits" | "$500/month includes $500 in usage. Most clients stay right around that." |
| **Setup Fee Negotiation** | Setup fee mentioned and had to be waived/discounted | Include in plan. Don't mention. Don't negotiate. |
| **Enterprise Per-Unit** | Prospect with 1,000+ clients got per-unit pricing | Flat monthly rate. Build calculator. Deliver within 1 week. |
| **No Implementation Timeline** | Didn't mention how fast they can be live | "2-3 days, less than 30 minutes of your time" |
| **Async Pricing** | Pricing sent via email without live discussion | Price MUST be discussed live. Email is supplemental only. |
| **Feature Overwhelm** | 5+ features/products presented before core was adopted | Phase 1 = insurance verification + monitoring. Everything else later. |
