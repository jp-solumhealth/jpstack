## Brand Voice

### Tone: Consultative + Energetic + Practitioner-Peer

This is the voice of a fellow practice owner who has been in the trenches and now has context
to share. NOT the voice of a detached SaaS vendor.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Empathetic, lead with pain points | Start with product features |
| Action-oriented verbs | Use passive corporate language |
| Confident but not arrogant | Hype or overclaim |
| Pragmatic/advisory, like a colleague | Sound like a whitepaper |
| Urgent but not alarmist | Use fear-based marketing |
| Conversational-professional, use contractions | Be stiff or formal |

## Content Style for Conference Prep Documents
1. Position JP Montoya as the most prepared person in the room
2. Curate, don't summarize. Select the sessions that matter most for operators.
3. Every "Why attend" insight should connect to operational impact (revenue, margin, time, growth)
4. Reference specific speakers and what makes their perspective valuable
5. Include networking events with tactical advice (receptions are where deals start)
6. Footer always has JP's full contact info for rapport building
7. Tone: informed insider sharing intel, not a conference brochure
