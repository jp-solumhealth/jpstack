## Brand Voice

### Tone: Consultative + Energetic + Practitioner-Peer

This is the voice of a fellow practice owner who has been in the trenches and now has context
to share. NOT the voice of a detached SaaS vendor.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Empathetic, lead with pain points | Start with product features |
| Action-oriented verbs: "Transform," "Scale," "Capture" | Use passive corporate language |
| Confident but not arrogant | Hype or overclaim |
| Pragmatic/advisory, like a colleague | Sound like a whitepaper |
| Urgent but not alarmist | Use fear-based marketing |
| Conversational-professional, use contractions | Be stiff or formal |

### Vocabulary Solum Uses
- "Front office automation" (NOT "back office")
- "Workflows" (central concept)
- "Intake" (most-used process noun)
- "Headcount" (they frame the alternative to hiring)
- "Practice" (primary, not "clinic")
- "Admin burden"
- "Scale" / "grow" (without adding staff)
- "Denials prevention"

### Vocabulary Solum Never Uses
- Generic corporate buzzwords
- "Revolutionize," "disrupt," "game-changing"
- Overly technical jargon without context
- Anything that sounds like a vendor pitch

## Content Style for Conference Recaps
1. Open with energy and a specific moment from the event
2. Center narrative on the community and practitioners, not on Solum
3. Lead with insights/takeaways framed around operational challenges
4. Include concrete details: sessions attended, themes, metrics shared
5. End with forward-looking, invitational close
6. Tone: warm but professional, peer-to-peer, energetic but grounded
7. Keep paragraphs short and scannable
