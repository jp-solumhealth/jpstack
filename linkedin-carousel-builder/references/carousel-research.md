# LinkedIn Carousel Research & Data

## Performance Benchmarks (2025-2026)

- Carousels get **1.6x more reach** than text-only posts on LinkedIn
- Document posts have **3.5x higher engagement rate** than image posts
- Average carousel completion rate: **38%** — aim for 60%+ (algorithm boost)
- Optimal slide count: **8-12 slides** (highest completion + engagement)
- Carousels with narrative arc get **2.2x more saves** than list-style
- Posts with carousels get **45% more comments** when ending with a question

## LinkedIn Document Viewer Behavior

- LinkedIn auto-crops to match the first slide's dimensions
- If slides have mixed dimensions, all are cropped to match slide 1
- Viewer overlays: page counter (~60px), zoom controls (~40px), navigation arrows (sides)
- On mobile: slides render at ~350px wide (1/3 of 1080px)
- PDF, PPTX, and DOCX all supported — PDF gives best quality control
- Max file size: 100MB, max pages: 300

## Safe Zone Specifications

Source: Multiple design tools (AI Carousels, Kapwing, PostNitro)

### 1080x1350 (4:5 Portrait - Recommended)
- Top dead zone: 0-80px
- Bottom dead zone: 1210-1350px (140px)
- Left dead zone: 0-80px
- Right dead zone: 1000-1080px (80px)
- Safe content area: 920x1130px

### Why 4:5 Portrait Wins
- Takes up more vertical space in mobile feed → harder to scroll past
- More real estate than square (1:1) for content
- Better aspect ratio for storytelling (top-to-bottom flow)
- Matches Instagram carousel format (cross-posting friendly)

## Color & Contrast for Mobile

- Minimum contrast ratio: 4.5:1 for body text (WCAG AA)
- Large text (24px+): 3:1 minimum
- White text on Navy (#011C40): contrast ratio ~17:1 (excellent)
- Navy text on white: contrast ratio ~17:1 (excellent)
- Teal (#70D3C6) on Navy: contrast ratio ~7:1 (good)
- Blue (#468AF7) on Navy: contrast ratio ~3.5:1 (borderline — use at 28px+)
- Purple (#A16CF4) on Navy: contrast ratio ~4:1 (use at 24px+)
- Avoid: light colors on light backgrounds, thin fonts at small sizes

## Hook Formulas That Convert

### Stat-Led (highest engagement)
"[Company] just [did X]. Here's what nobody is talking about."
"[Metric] in [timeframe]. I broke down how."

### Contrarian
"Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong. Here's why."
"The worst advice in [industry]: [common advice]."

### Story Arc
"[Person/Company] went from [bad state] to [good state]. The pivot nobody expected."
"5 years ago, [setup]. Today, [surprising outcome]."

### Curiosity Gap
"I analyzed [X] and found [surprising pattern]."
"There's one thing [successful people] do differently."

## Slide-by-Slide Engagement Data

- Slide 1: 100% view rate (the hook)
- Slide 2: ~85% continue (biggest drop-off point)
- Slide 3: ~72% continue
- Slide 4-6: ~65% (if they reach slide 3, most finish)
- Last slide: ~55-60% typical, 70%+ for high-performing carousels

**Key insight**: The battle is won or lost between slides 1-3. Front-load value.

## Document Title Best Practices

The document title appears in LinkedIn's viewer and can drive clicks:
- Keep under 60 characters
- Include a number or metric if possible
- Create curiosity or promise value
- Examples:
  - "$835M Comeback: From 96% Crash to Biggest Deal of 2026"
  - "7 Pricing Mistakes That Kill Healthcare Startups"
  - "The Playbook Behind 200M Insured Lives"
